The application

Filing it, online or by mail.

Both paths use the same $100 fee and ask for the documentation above. Online is faster.

A

Apply online (faster)

Recommended

Register, renew, or update through the Attorney General's online system (PA-HICRS). On approval, the system emails the unique Pennsylvania Home Improvement Contractor number (PA#) and the expiration date. An application returns one of three outcomes: approved, pended, or denied.

Register online
B

Apply by mail

Alternative

Complete the paper application in ink, every section, and send it with a $100 check or money order payable to the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. Allow several weeks; certificates arrive by mail within about two to three weeks unless the application is incomplete or incorrect.

C

After you are registered

Ongoing

Put the PA# on all advertising, contracts, estimates, and proposals. Include the Bureau of Consumer Protection line (1-888-520-6680) and the statement "Registration does not imply endorsement." Renew every two years. Anyone can verify a registration through the public HIC search.

Public HIC search HICPA §§ 4, 6, 7
Why the disclosures matter. The statute lets the Bureau deny registration for convictions tied to home improvement fraud, fraud, theft, or deception. An accurate, complete application is what keeps the outcome at "approved" rather than "pended" or "denied." If anything is uncertain, confirm it against the official instructions PDF linked above before submitting.
