Act on it

The official state portals, built to move you through licensure.

Pennsylvania has put the registration and licensing process into a small set of government systems. Start with the One-Stop Shop, which generates a personalized checklist of the exact licenses and permits this business needs, then use the agency portals to file.

Start here: the PA Business One-Stop Shop

The Commonwealth's One-Stop Shop "asks basic questions about your business to provide a list of the licenses, permits, forms and contact information you will need." It distills business startup into a personalized checklist you can save in the Hub. This is the state-built pathway geared specifically to facilitate registration and licensure, so it is the right first stop before filing anything.

Agency portals

Where each step actually gets filed.

Each portal below is the official government system for that step. They are ordered to match the pathway.

Step 1 · 2

PA Department of State — Business Filings

Form the LLC (Certificate of Organization) and register a fictitious name. Online filing through the Business Filing Services portal.

Open the filing portal
Step 3

IRS — Employer Identification Number

Apply for a free EIN directly from the IRS once the entity exists. Issued immediately online.

Apply for an EIN
Step 4

PA Department of Revenue — myPATH

Register state tax accounts, including a sales-tax license if needed and employer withholding if hiring.

Register at myPATH
Step 5

Office of Attorney General — HIC Registration

Register as a Home Improvement Contractor. This is the central residential requirement, renewed every two years.

Register / renew
Step 7

EPA — Lead-Safe Firm Certification (RRP)

Certify the firm to perform renovation, repair, or painting in pre-1978 housing. Valid five years.

Certify the firm
Step 8

PA Dept. of Labor & Industry — Workers' Comp

Employer information and coverage obligations once the business has one or more employees.

Workers' comp portal
Step 11

PA Uniform Construction Code (UCC)

Find how the UCC is enforced locally and where to pull building permits and inspections for the work municipality.

UCC home
Step 11 · municipal license

City contractor licenses

Where an actual general contractor license exists. Confirm the rules for the exact municipality of each job; these two are the largest programs.

Pittsburgh PLI Philadelphia L&I
How to use these together. Run the One-Stop checklist first so the state tells you which of these portals apply to this specific business and location. Then work the agency portals in pathway order. The printable checklist tracks the same steps offline.

Not legal advice. Portal links point to official Pennsylvania and federal government systems, confirmed in June 2026. Government URLs and processes change; if a link moves, search the agency's name plus the program. Confirm municipal licensing for the contractor's actual service area.
